ViDi: A Benchmark for the Identification and Captioning of Visual Differences in Image Pairs

Paper Data

ViDi pipeline summary.

Abstract

Recent advances in Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) have brought forward capabilities such as handling multiple images or engage in multi-turn conversations involving images.

In this paper, we present a challenging spot the differences benchmark for evaluating MLLMs, geared toward the identification and captioning of Visual Differences (ViDi). The benchmark corresponds to a test-only dataset with 200 pairs of images, along with human-annotated descriptions of visually noticeable differences for each pair. ViDi goes beyond the identification of single differences, challenging models to articulate changes using natural language, while pinpointing the subject of each difference and its absolute or relative location. Empirical results reveal that MLLMs are still in an early development stage, showing limited performance in spot the differences tasks.

Zero-shot Eval on ViDi Benchmark

Setup. We evaluated prominent MLLMs at generating descriptions of multiple diffirences between pairs of images.

Granular Alignment: Models describe a single difference, evaluated by matching against individual ground-truth descriptions.

Global Alignment: Models list all differences, assessed against the combined ground-truth descriptions.

Metrics: BLEU, ROUGE_L, and CIDEr used to measure text generation quality, coherence, and relevance to annotations.

Consistency: Evaluated by comparing performance when swapping the input image order.

How do MLLMs perform on ViDi?

Performance significantly drops in the global alignment setting, indicating difficulty in comprehensively capturing all differences. Models exhibit repetitive outputs and struggle with generating accurate, complete descriptions. InternVL2.5-78B model performs best at granular alignment, showcasing a capacity for localized difference detection. The task of finding one difference is more manageable by current MLLMs, whereas the more complex task of comprehensively describing differences is generally not accomplished.

ViDi provides a challenging, realistic benchmark that pushes MLLM evaluation beyond simplified scenarios.

Results underscore the gap between linguistic fluency and true visual comprehension in current MLLMs.
